COMP3111: Introduction to Software Engineering
Spring 2015

Important Course Notes and Policies

  1. Instructional approach
    The lectures will cover the basic textbook material. Lectures will not necessarily cover all of the indicated reading, so do not skip the readings. As a general principle, you will be responsible for the topics covered in lectures and for assigned sections of the textbook. In this course, you will learn as much by doing (in the tutorials, labs and project) as by listening (in the lectures). This will probably be the first time that you will produce a software product rather than simply a program. The greatest challenge you will face, and the greatest benefit derived, will be from the experience of working in a large group and producing the required artifacts (i.e., documentation and code) according to a strict schedule. Because you are expected to take a very active role in this course, you must come to each lecture prepared, which means reading the assigned chapters before the lecture and identifying questions that you need answered. Preparation also means being willing to find the information that you need, for example, in textbooks, on the Web, or in other resources.

  2. Expected work load.
    This course will take up quite a lot of your time this semester. From previous experience, project-oriented software engineering courses are the heaviest workload courses in both graduate and undergraduate curricula. However, good planning and time scheduling can greatly mitigate your workload.

  3. Lecture notes
    Lecture notes are available from the course home page. Lecture notes are outlines only of the lecture material. They are not a substitute for the textbook and should not be relied on solely for your knowledge of the course material. In particular, they are not a substitute for reading the indicated chapters in the textbook or for attending lectures.

  4. Project milestones out/due dates.
    The project milestone due dates are firm and will not be changed unless exceptional circumstances arise.

  5. Project
    The course project is a group project broken down to required activities. The project description is available online. The instructor will form groups during the first week of lectures from students enrolled in the course. The instructor reserves the right to remove members from or to add members to any group at any time during the semester so as to more evenly balance the number of people in each group.

  6. Labs/Tutorials
    The tutorials will be used to introduce you to various software tools that you will need to use to do the project (primarily, Eclipse Java Platform and MySQL Database Engine). The labs will be used for practice exercises related to the tutorial material. You need to successfully complete the lab assignment during the lab session to get tutorial and lab mark. The labs and tutorials are also a time during which you can work with your group members on the project or consult with a TA about the project or other course material.

  7. Academic conduct.
    Unless otherwise stated, the work that you submit for grading must represent the result of independent effort (individual for tests, group/team for the project). In particular, you are not allowed to give solutions to or to copy solutions from other individuals/groups. Suspected breaches of these rules will be referred to the relevant University authorities for disciplinary action. If in doubt, consult with an instructor or a TA first.

  8. Computer account security.
    Remember, do not share your account with anyone and Protect your CSE account password!

  9. Classroom etiquette.
    The following rules are for the benefit of all to ensure that the classroom environment is conducive to teaching and learning.
    • You are expected to arrive on time for lectures (as is the instructor ) and generally not to leave during the lecture unless an emergency arises. If you must leave during a lecture, please do so as quietly as possible with minimum disruption to the class.
    • Talking is strictly prohibited during the lecture unless you are asked a question by the instructor or directed to discuss something amongst yourselves. To behave otherwise shows disrespect, not only for the instructor, but more importantly for your fellow students.
    Please be considerate in your behavior and consider the possible effects of your actions on others before acting.

  10. Information posted on the course web pages
    Please note that the course web pages are constantly undergoing revision. No information on the course web pages is final until it has been announced in class.
