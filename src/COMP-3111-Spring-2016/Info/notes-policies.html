COMP 3111: Software Engineering

Course Notes and Policies

  1. Instructional Approach
    In this course, you will learn both by listening (in the lectures) and by doing (in the tutorials, labs and project); both are important. The lectures will be used to cover the basic concepts and techniques of software engineering. The tutorials and labs will be used to allow you to apply the concepts and techniques to the development of a software product using industry standard software development tools.

  2. Expected Work Load
    This course may take up quite a lot of your time this semester since project-oriented software engineering courses are usually the heaviest workload courses in both graduate and undergraduate curricula. However, prior planning and good time management can greatly mitigate your workload.

  3. Lecture Notes
    The lecture notes are available from the course website on Canvas. Lecture notes are outlines only of the lecture material. They are not a substitute for attending lectures.

  4. Course Project
    The course project is both an individual and a team project. For the team part of the project, you will form your own teams from students enrolled in the course. However, the instructor reserves the right to remove members from or to add members to a team so as to more evenly balance the number of people in each team.

    A project activity is due by the due date and time specified for the activity. Late submissions will not be accepted.

    This will probably be the first time that you will produce a software product rather than simply a program. The greatest challenge you will face, and the greatest benefit derived, will be from the experience both of working in a team and of producing the required project artifacts (i.e., documentation, code and test cases) according to a strict schedule.

  5. Tutorials/Labs
    The tutorials will be used primarily to introduce you to various software tools that you will need to do the project (i.e., Visual Studio, ASP.NET, SQL Server, Git, Nunit, and Rational Modeler).

    The labs will be used to work on the project and to do formal testing of your software. Doing the lab activities will form part of your final grade for the course.

    When not used for specific instruction or activities, the tutorials and labs are also a time during which you can work on the project or consult with a TA about the project or other course material.

  6. Academic Integrity
    Unless otherwise stated, the work that you submit for grading must represent the result of independent effort. In particular, you are not allowed to give solutions to or to copy solutions from other individuals/teams. Suspected breaches of this rule will be referred to the relevant university authorities for disciplinary action. If in doubt, consult with the instructor or a TA first.

  7. Computer Account Security
    Do not share your account with anyone and always protect your CSE account password!

  8. Classroom Etiquette
    The following rules are for everyone's benefit to ensure that the classroom environment is conducive to teaching and learning.
    • You are expected to arrive on time for lectures (as is the instructor ) and generally not to leave during the lecture unless an emergency arises. If you must leave during a lecture, please do so as discretely and quietly as possible so as to give minimum disruption to the class.
    • Talking is strictly prohibited during the lecture unless the instructor asks you a question or directs you to discuss something amongst yourselves. To behave otherwise shows disrespect, not only for the instructor, but more importantly for your fellow students.
    Please be considerate in your behavior and consider the possible effects of your actions on others before acting.

  9. Course Web Pages
    The course web pages are constantly undergoing revision. No information on the course web pages is final until it has been announced or confirmed in a lecture.
