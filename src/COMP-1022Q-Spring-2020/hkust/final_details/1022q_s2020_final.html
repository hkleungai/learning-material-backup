  • Your camera must show:

  • To make sure you are ready for the exam, you need to get into the meeting room at least 20 mins before the start of the exam

Your Computer Desktop

  • You can only use these things during the exam: Zoom, Excel, and a browser a browser and/or your document viewer
  • You need to close all other programs
  • There is no communication with others
  • If you want to use a tablet to view the notes such as using an iPad, you have to make the device visible within the live video

⚠ Summary of Important Things You Need to Know about the Zoom Meetings ⚠

  • The Zoom meetings will start around 7:30am on the day so you can get ready super early
  • You must go to the Zoom meeting that you are assigned to (penalty will be applied if you fail to do so)
  • You must be in the Zoom meeting 10 minutes before the start of the exam, i.e. you must be in the Zoom meeting before 8:20am (penalty will be applied if you fail to do so)
  • You must change your profile name to the one that is shown on your Student ID in the meeting (penalty will be applied if you fail to do so)
  • You must show a live video of yourself and your computer as described above (penalty will be applied if you fail to do so)
    • If you are not sure about the correctness of your video, join the meeting early and make sure it is okay
    • You need to plug in your mobile, notebook and everything to make sure battery is full all the time!
  • You must not use virtual background in your video (penalty will be applied if you fail to do so)
  • You need to make sure your Student ID or Personal ID is ready
  • You need to know how to share your screen before the exam, as we may ask you to do it in the breakout room
    • Zoom help for sharing screen here
  • You need to make sure your microphone is working, as we may unmute it in the breakout room
  • You must follow usual exam rules such as no talking and no messaging (you will get an F for the course immediately if you do that)

The Content of the Final Exam

  • The exam will have approximately 20~30 questions
  • You will work on the exam online inside a canvas quiz
  • Some questions are multiple-choice questions and some are short questions
    • For example, you could be shown a worksheet with some content, shown some VBA code which makes the content, and then asked to write the missing parts of the code
  • The exam could include questions about anything that has been discussed on the course (lecture or lab)
  • The material covered in the midterm exam could be included in the final exam

How Does the Final Exam Differ to the Midterm Exam?

  • The general style of the exam will be the same as the midterm
  • However, the final exam is obviously longer than the midterm exam
  • Final exam questions are likely to be longer than those in the midterm
  • For example, we may show longer sections of VBA code, and ask you what happens

A Good Way to Revise

  • A good way to revise is:
    • make sure you understand all the notes
    • make sure you understand all the lab work
      • Try changing them and see how the behaviour changes
    • go through all the course examples, run them, and check you understand them
      • Try changing them and see how the behaviour changes
    • go through the practice questions and check you understand them
