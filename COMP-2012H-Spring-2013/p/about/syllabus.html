Grading

The breakdown is subject to change as a whole and adjustments on a per-student basis in exceptional cases. This is the general breakdown we'll be using:

Projects: 50%
Lab assignments: 5%
Midterm Exam: 15% Mar 26, 2013 in class
Written assignments: 5%
Final Exam: 25%

Written assignemtnts are short exercises given on a weekly (or biweekly) basis after the midterm exam, and these exercises are selected from the Weiss' book. The midterm and final are closed book. Skipping the final examination without prior approval will automatically lead to an "F" grade for the course.

Projects:

CS 2012h is taught using the Lab 2 (4214) consisting of roughly 40 Lenovo ThinkCenter M58 Intel C2D E8400 3.0GHz CPU (EM64T) machines, with Intel Q45 Chipset, 4GB RAM, 160GB Harddisk, and 17" LCD Color Monitors. The lab is open 24 hours (the only lab in UST that is open round the clock; use your student ID to access the lab) and can be reached by lift 19.

All programming assignments will be done individually except Project #2 where students can choose to work in pairs and submit one project. There will be five projects and their tentative are listed:

Project #1: Paint (Mar 8)
A mini-version of the Microsoft Paint, completed with a graphical user interface (GUI).
Project #2: Tetris (Mar 28):
An interactive 2D game, completed with a GUI.
Project #3: Deques and randomized queues (Arp 12):
A data structure project on queue objects.
Project #4: Pattern recognition (Apr 26):
Line detection using brute-force and sorting.
Project #5: Course registration system (May 16):
A complex application based on linked-list objects.
For projects 1, 2, 5, you will have approximately three weeks for each project. Projects 3 and 4 are less involved and you will have one to two weeks for each of them.

Your project will first be electronically tested for cheating before grading to make sure that it conforms to the "no-cheating" policy. Occasionally, you will be contacted by the TA to demo your project, who will then quiz you to determine how well you understand the structure of the code, the design trade-offs, and the implemented algorithms.

You may discuss the projects with your classmates, but only at the methodological level without going into details.  Unless specified otherwise, you must do them all by yourself.  Copying the work of someone else in entirety or in part is strictly prohibited.  You will also be penalized if you show your work to others.  Suspected cheating and plagiarism will be referred to the University authorities for investigation and appropriate disciplinary actions.  (References: Academic Integrity and Discipline, A Framework on Academic Integrity and Conduct in the Classroom).

Students taking CS 2012h acknowledge that if sufficient evidence are available indicating that they are involved in cheating in projects, they are subject to the following penalties:

  • first-time offender: zero in project, minus one letter subgrade.
  • second-time offender: F (no further discussion)

Projects Turn-in & Late Policy:

Projects are due at 23:59:00 on the due date. You are permitted a total of five late days without penalty for the semester that may be used on any of the five programming projects. After that, the grade on the late projects are marked down at a rate of 33% per day, meaning that if you fail to turn in an assignment on time it is worth 66% for the first 24 hours after the deadline, 33% for the next 24 hours, and it is worth nothing after that.

Exceptions will be given only in extreme circumstances and only in advance.

Lab assignments:

Labs are taught in Lab 2 (4214). There will be 12 labs. Lab assignments are to be submitted and demoed to TA in each lab session. Alternatively you can choose to submit your labs once and for all in batch mode.
