Scope of the exam:
The exam will cover everything that we have studied this so far semester in class up to and including the material that will be covered on Thursday Sept 29.  This includes everything taught in class, including those extra things written on the board/transparencies that do not appear on the slides.  Please note that you are also responsible for material that was covered in the book that was not covered in the slides.

***NEW***
The exam will cover the materials in Lectures 1-5 presented in class and everything in the book from pages 1-71 with the exception of pp. 28-41 (section 1.4).  Please note that this means that the exam definitely covers the extended GCD algorithm and how to calculate inverses
 ***NEW***

How to prepare for the exam:
Make sure that you can answer all of the questions on the homeworks correctly, and understand why they are correct.  We will not ask the exact same questions but some of the exam questions will be similar to the homework questions.

Practice by trying to work out the questions at the end of each of the textbook's sections, especially the ones that provide hints. If you want to know if your solutions are correct,  please make appointments with the TAs to discuss the solutions

Practice writing out explanations.  Solutions without clear explanations will loose a lot of points on the exam

Again,  go talk with the TAs.  There are 8 TAs and they are all available to talk with you.  Send them email (their email is available on the class web-site) to schedule an appointment with one.  I highly recommend that you do this BEFORE Monday October 3.

There will be two supplementary review sessions before the exam, to answer any questions you might have.
Tuesday, September 27, 2005: LTF  given by the instructor
Thursday,  September 29, 2005, Room 3007, given by the TA, Mr Leung Yiu Cho

Finally,  please note that there will be NO class during the normal L1 and L2 lecture hours on Tuesday October 4.  Instead, the lecturer will be in the classroom to answer any questions you might have


Miscellaneous:
o The exam will be closed book (no notes allowed)
o Calculators are allowed for use during the exam (but are not really necessary)

Scope of the exam:
The exam will cover everything that we have studied this semester. This means that everything covered in the slides numbered Lectures 1-18 (and the associated material in the book and tutorials) can be asked on the exam 
The only exceptions to this are the two small sections of the slides that we did not cover which are
(i) In Lecture 15, you do not need to know the 3rd section "The Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion for Counting" (note that you are responsible for the first two subsections of Lecture 15, just not for the third).  In particular,  you do need to know "Inclusion-Exclusion for Probability".
(ii) In Lecture 18,  you do not need to know the material after page 31 (which is the page that says that "material form this point on, will not be on the exam").
(iii) Lecture 19 (Intro to Graphs) will NOT be on the exam
 


As mentioned, you also need to know all of the material covered in the handouts available in the "other" column on the Notes page.  The only exception to this is that
(i) You do not need to know the handout on Floors & Ceilings in Divide and Conquer which is in the "other" column for 10/11/05
 

With that said,  please note that  the majority of the questions on the exam (approximately 75%) will be on material covered AFTER the  2nd midterm (this ensures that the three tests, taken together,  give approximately equal weighting to all material taught this semester).
