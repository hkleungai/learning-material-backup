Exam Seating

Due to the large number of students in the class,  the midterms will be held in multiple venues at the same time.  You will be assigned the venue in which you should write the exam. A few days before each exam is held,  we will post the venue assignments to the class web page.

 

Study Tips for the exam

  • Look at old exams
  • Make sure that you understand solutions to all of the homeworks and supplementary questions.
    • We will not ask the same questions on the exams but we might ask some similar ones
  • Practice writing explanations of solutions.
    • We will be deducting points for solutions without justifications
  • Try practicing on problems in back of the book chapters.
    • If you have trouble, go see the TAs (any TA, not just yours). They know answers to all of these problems.
  • For copyright reasons Homework Solutions can only be accessed from WITHIN the UST internet domain.
    • If you live or study off-campus, make sure that you download the solutions before you leave campus.

Other Comments

  • Exams will be closed-book
    • but non-programmable calculators can be used.  By a non-programmable calculator we mean a calculator that can't be programmed to run the algorithms we learnt in class,  e.g., the extended GCD algorithm.  A standard scientific calculator with many pre-defined functions is perfectly acceptable.
       
  • New Calculator policy (modified May 28, 2010)
    • After many years (and much technological progress) we have decided to change our exam calculator policy.  The only functions needed for  the exam are the basic arithmetic ones.  So,  from now on , only two types of calculators will be allowed on the exam:  H.K.E.A.A. approved calculators and  "Simple" calculators".  If you would like to use a calculator that does not fall into one of these categories,  you need to receive proir approval from your instructor (send him/her a picture of your calculator with your request).  A list of approved calculators will be mainained below
      • H.K.E.A.A. approved calculators.
        As their name implies, these are calculators approved for use by the H.K.E.A.A.. If the calculator does NOT have an HKEAA Approved  or HKEA APPROVED label on it, then you need to document its approval.  You can do this by printing out this list and circling your calculator's model #.  Bring the page to the exam and show it and your calculator to the TA who is doing the exam sign-in.
      • Simple Calculators
        For the purposes of COMP170  a "simple calculator" is one whose display is one character high.  That is, the calculator display can display one row of alphanumeric data but no more. As examples
        • this simple calculator http://web.uvic.ca/~chem102/Calculator.jpg  could be brought into the exam.
        • this graphing calculator http://www.wwu.edu/depts/tutorialcenter/images/calculator.gif could not.
      • Other preapproved calculators
        Casio FX-991ES,  Casio FX-991ES PLUS, Casio FX-82MS, Casio FX-991MS,Casio FX-82TL, Casio FX-570MS.
