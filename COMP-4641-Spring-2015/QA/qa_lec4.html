Proof of clustering coefficients $C=\frac{3}{4}$ in High clustering High diameter case:

First thing to note is, it is not $$ \lim_{N\to\infty}C = \frac{3}{4} $$ but $$ \lim_{k\to\infty}\lim_{N\to\infty}C = \frac{3}{4} $$

So when $N\to\infty$, consider node $n_0$ and its $k$ neighbors (suppose $k$ is even value) $$ \{n_i\}, i \in \{ -\frac{k}{2}, \cdots, -1, 1, \cdots, \frac{k}{2} \} $$

Now we need to count how many edges among these $k$ nodes, to not repeatedly count edges between 2 nodes, we only count how many edges a node has with its right-side nodes.

For nodes $\{n_i\}, i \in \{-\frac{k}{2},\cdots,-1\}$, each of them has $\frac{k}{2}-1$ edges with its right-side nodes.

For nodes $\{n_i\}, i \in \{1,\cdots,\frac{k}{2}\}$, from node $n_1$ to node $n_{\frac{k}{2}}$, their right-side edges will decrease from $\frac{k}{2}-1$ to $0$.

Thus total edges within its neighbors are $$ \frac{k}{2}(\frac{k}{2} - 1) + \frac{\frac{k}{2}(\frac{k}{2} - 1)}{2} = \frac{3}{4}k(\frac{k}{2} - 1)$$ So the clustering coefficient is $$ \lim_{k\to\infty}\lim_{N\to\infty}C = \lim_{k\to\infty }\frac{\frac{3}{4}k(\frac{k}{2} - 1)}{\frac{1}{2}k(k-1)} = \frac{3}{4} $$
