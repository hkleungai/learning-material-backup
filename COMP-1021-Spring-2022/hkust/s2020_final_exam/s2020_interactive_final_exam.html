  • exactly divisible by 2, and

  • exactly divisible by 7, and
  • exactly divisible by 10

?

For example, the number 140 is one of those numbers, because:

  • it is in the range 100, 101, ... , 498, 499
  • it is exactly divisible by 2 (140/2=70), and
  • it is exactly divisible by 5 (140/5=28), and
  • it is exactly divisible by 7 (140/7=20), and
  • it is exactly divisible by 10 (140/10=14)

For example, the number 150 is not one of those numbers, because:

  • it is in the range 100, 101, ... , 498, 499

  • it is exactly divisible by 5 (150/5=30), but
  • it is not exactly divisible by 7 (150/7=21.429) so it is not one of those numbers
  • it is exactly divisible by 10 (150/10=15)

In total, how many numbers in the range 100, 101, ... , 498, 499 are exactly divisible by 2, 5, 7 and 10 in the way described above?
