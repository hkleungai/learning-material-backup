Course Info

 

Inner workings of modern digital computer systems and tradeoffs at the hardware-software interface. Topics include: instructions set design, memory systems, input-output systems, interrupts and exceptions, pipelining, performance and cost analysis, assembly language programming, and a survey of advanced architectures.
 
Prerequisites:

COMP 1002 or COMP 1004/1004H or COMP 2011. Exclusion: ELEC 2300

Reading Materials:

  • Textbook

David A. Patterson & John L. Hennessy (2012). Computer Organization and Design: The Hardware/Software Interface. Revised. Fourth Edition. Morgan Kaufmann. 

  • References

- Robert L. Britton (2003). MIPS Assembly Language Programming. Prentice Hall.

- MARS MIPS simulator tutorial.
