COMP1022Q Lab 4 Marking Scheme

Marking Scheme

  1. Using Excel formulas to calculate the bullet path (30 marks)
    • Correct X (column A) values (5 marks)
    • Correct Y (column B) formulas (10 marks)
    • Correct Column (column C) formulas (5 marks)
    • Correct Row (column D) formulas (10 marks)
  2. The first macro - setting the target (30 marks)
    • The macro is triggered by Ctrl-Shift-T
    • When the macro is triggered:
      • First, the background colour of every cell in the worksheet is set to white (10 marks)
      • Then the target cell is shown randomly, somewhere in the first 40 columns and the first 40 rows (10 marks)
        • Make sure that the target cell is within the range A1:AN40
        • That's because AN is the 40th column
      • A red background colour is used for the target cell (10 marks)
  3. The second macro - shooting the bullet (40 marks)
    • The macro is triggered by Ctrl-Shift-S
    • A random colour index ranging from 4 (Green) to 18 (Purple) is used (10 marks)
    • In a loop, you should have the following:
      • Bullet row and column values are retrieved from the Calculations Worksheet, and the background colour of the appropriate cells are changed to show the path of the bullet (10 marks)
      • The loop finishes early (using Exit For) if the bullet is too high or too low (10 marks)
      • If the bullet hits the target, show "Well done! You have hit the target!" (or similar text) and then finish the loop (using Exit For) (10 marks)
  4. Enter your name and student ID number somewhere at/near the top of column D. In the lab, Cell D1 is recommended
    • This is your statement that the work is yours

Late Submission Policy

  • If you submit late, it causes us a lot of trouble, so please don't do that
  • The submission link won't work after your lab deadline
  • If you are late, you will have to send your work in an email to cspeter@cse.ust.hk with a suitable email title such as "COMP1022Q Late Lab Submission"
  • Each lab project is given a mark out of 100
  • At least 20 marks will be deducted if you submit late
  • For every 12 hours (or part of that) you are late, 20 marks are deducted. For example,
    • If you are late for 1 second, 20 marks will be deducted
    • If you are late for 11 hours and 59 minutes, 20 marks will be deducted
    • If you are late for 12 hours and 1 minute, 40 marks will be deducted
    • and so on
