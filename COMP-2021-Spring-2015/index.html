Course Information

    Course Descriptions

    Unix utilities and file structure; links and symbolic links; data processing and process control in the Unix shell; Shell programming; regular expressions; script programming in the Unix environment. Laboratory exercises are designed to give hands-on practice with software tools and to increase programmer productivity.

    Prerequisites

    COMP 1002 OR COMP 1004/104H (prior to 2009-10) OR COMP 1021 OR COMP 1022P OR COMP 1022Q; OR a passing grade in HKALE Computer Studies or HKCEE Computer Studies; OR level 3 or above in HKDSE Information and Communication Technology.

Notes
  • The project can be of any topic. Final codes should contain at least 50% of Shell script, Perl or CGI.
  • Presentations will be on Apr 23 & 30 in class. All students are required to attend both project presentation sessions. 1 point from final score will be deducted if you fail to attend any of the sessions.
  • The project submission deadline is May 8 5pm. You need to submit your project report, PowerPoint slides, project video (if any) and source code via CASS. One submission for each group. Make sure you have student ID, name and email for each group member in the cover page of the report.
  • All students can vote for "Best 3" projects. The final decision is made by instructor and teaching team. Team members of "Best 3" project earn a bonus 2 points to their final score.
