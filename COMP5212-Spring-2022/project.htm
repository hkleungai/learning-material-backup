As can be seen from those examples, a term project typically involves materials covered late in a course or even out of the scope of the course. You will need to pick up such materials by yourself. There are plenty of materials on Machine Learning online. Taking a course provides you an opportunity to learn about a subject. It does not mean that you have to learn everything from the instructor. Click here for the videos and lecture notes of a previous offering of this course.

 

You are expected to work in groups of 3 to 4 students. You need to form your group and decide on a project to work on by February 25.  Register your group and project at this Google Doc. Use Piazza to find groupmate if necessary.

 

The project report, to be written in a conference paper format, is due on May 11, along with your code and the datasets that you use. You will also need to submit a 15-minute video via Youtube.  Detailed submission instructions will be given later.  The grading will be mostly based on the video. In your report, clearly state the contributions by each group member. Everyone is expected to do some coding and/or experiments, and to do a part of the presentation.  Acknowledge all resources that you use.

 

Grading scheme: There are totally 28 points, which are broken down as follows

 

¡¤        8: Coherence --- Does the project has a clear and coherent objective? How interesting and coherent are the results? Any insightful discussions?

¡¤        10: Effort ---- How much work is involved?   Is the project too simplistic or does it require a substantial amount of work? Is the amount of work by this group drastically less than the past projects posted on the course website?

¡¤        5: Difficulty --- How challenging is the project technically?

¡¤        5: Video and Report --- Is the oral presentation smooth and easily understood? Does the report read well?

 

Include an individual self-assessment in your report.  Give a score that you think you deserve for each of the above four categories, and explain why.

 

Double Submission: The use of the same work for multiple courses is strictly prohibited. If your project is related to another project from another course, clearly state the differences.
